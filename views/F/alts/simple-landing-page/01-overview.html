Data saves lives

Your health data is very important to the NHS. It is shared between doctors, hospitals and dentists for reasons linked to your direct care.

Your personally identifiable information is also shared beyond your direct care for research and planning purposes. This helps to improve health and social care services for you and your family. The NHS depends on this information to plan essential facilities, like heart units and specialist care wards. The data can also be used in research to improve treatment for serious conditions and disease.

Understanding health data

Whenever you visit an NHS service, we use and update a record of information all about you. This is known as your Summary Care Record (SCR) and contains your patient health information.

Find out more about health data

Sharing your data

An organisation that collects and uses identifiable information is known as a data controller. NHS Digital is a data controller.

Learn more about how your data is shared

Benefits of data sharing

Your patient health information is shared between doctors, hospitals and dentists for reasons linked to your own direct care. There is also huge potential to use patient health information to improve health and social care services across the NHS.

Learn more about the benefits of data sharing

How data is kept secure

The NHS takes the responsibility for protecting your personally identifiable information very seriously. Your data is important for your direct care and for improving health and social care services in England.

Find out how your data is kept secure

How opting out works

There are some circumstances where opt-outs do not apply and your personally identifiable data will be used regardless. This includes anytime you visit a health or social care professional for your own direct care purposes.

Learn how the opt out process works

Registering your preference

You now have the chance to opt-out of sharing your personally identifiable information for purposes beyond your direct care. This means it will not be shared for reasons linked to research or planning.

Use this online service to register your preferences for sharing your patient data. By sharing your data you can contribute to the health of future generations.

Reducing the length of stay for breast cancer surgery patients

Sharing data has many positive effects. The length of time spent in hospital for breast cancer surgery patients dropped massively back in 2012. This was all thanks to relevant data sharing. The average length of hospital stay was reduced by 50 per cent.
